Favorite Athlete

Stephen Curry

Stephen Curry changed modern basketball through shooting range, off-ball movement, and ball-handling rhythm. What fascinates me most is not only the three-point shot, but the way he turns spacing, judgment, and confidence into a light-looking style that is extremely hard to copy.

Career

Career Journey

Curry was born in 1988 and played college basketball for the Davidson Wildcats. During the 2008 NCAA Tournament, he led Davidson to the Elite Eight and drew national attention as a guard who could repeatedly dismantle defenses with shooting despite not having an overpowering frame.

The Golden State Warriors selected Curry with the 7th overall pick in the 2009 NBA Draft. Early ankle injuries raised doubts about his future, but he gradually shaped deep threes, quick releases, pick-and-roll decisions, and constant off-ball movement into a complete offensive ecosystem.

In 2014-15, Curry won his first regular-season MVP and led the Warriors to the championship. In 2015-16, he became the first unanimous MVP in NBA history. In 2022, he led Golden State to another title and won Finals MVP, adding the most important missing piece to his resume.
