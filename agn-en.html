Research Direction

Active Galactic Nuclei / Changing-Look AGN

Active galactic nuclei are powered by accretion onto supermassive black holes at the centers of galaxies. They connect accretion disks, emission-line regions, dusty structures, jets, and host galaxies.

Unified Model

The Unified Model of AGN

The unified model explains different AGN classes through a common central engine: a supermassive black hole, an accretion disk, fast gas producing broad emission lines, larger-scale narrow-line gas, and obscuring dusty material.

Observed differences depend not only on intrinsic physics, but also on viewing angle, obscuration, jet orientation, accretion rate, and the host environment. A direct view of the broad-line region can produce a Type 1 spectrum; an obscured view may reveal mainly narrow emission lines.

Changing-Look AGN

What Are Changing-Look AGN?

Spectral Transitions

Changing-look AGN can show broad emission lines appearing or disappearing between observing epochs.

Accretion Changes

A major explanation is a large change in accretion rate, which changes the continuum and the broad-line response.

Obscuration Changes

Another possibility is variable dust or gas obscuration along the line of sight, tested with spectra, light curves, and multiwavelength data.

Multiwavelength

AGN Across the Spectrum

Radio

Radio

Radio observations trace relativistic jets, compact cores, and galaxy-scale outflows, helping separate jet-dominated and accretion-dominated energy output.

Image Source: NASA
IR

Infrared

Dust absorbs central radiation and re-emits it in the infrared, making IR data especially important for obscured AGN and dusty structures.

Image Source: ESA/Webb
Optical / UV

Optical and UV

Optical and UV continua, broad lines, and narrow lines reveal ionized gas, accretion-disk variability, and host-galaxy components.

Image Source: Chandra/HST
X-ray

X-ray

X-rays originate from high-energy regions close to the black hole and probe the corona, absorbing column, and rapid variability.

Image Source: Chandra
Gamma-ray

Gamma-ray

For blazars whose jets point near our line of sight, gamma-ray spectra reveal extreme particle acceleration and relativistic effects.

Image Source: NASA/Fermi
Time

Variability

Multi-epoch photometric and spectroscopic monitoring connects continuum changes, broad-line responses, and accretion-state transitions.

Image Source: NASA/Fermi
